# Engineering Design Brief: Gearbox Model

## Context

The project responds to a classroom learning need: students often learn gear ratios and gearbox behaviour as abstract diagrams, even though the concepts are easier to understand when the gear train is visible and physically interactive.

## Intended Users

- Secondary school students learning systems engineering or mechanical concepts.
- Teachers who need a low-cost demonstration model for gear ratios, output speed, and synchroniser behaviour.
- Students reviewing how design decisions change after fabrication and diagnostic testing.

## Design Objective

Design and construct a model gearbox that demonstrates how gear pairs and a synchroniser can change output speed. The model should be clear enough for learning use, affordable enough for a school context, and safe enough for classroom demonstration.

## Requirements And Criteria

- Demonstrate visible gear rotation on shafts.
- Allow gear-speed changes through a synchroniser-style mechanism.
- Use a mix of 3D printed parts, wood, bearings, dowels, and low-cost workshop materials.
- Keep external purchased materials close to the report's $30 budget target.
- Stay stable during classroom demonstration.
- Reduce safety risks from finger jams, electrical faults, falling hazards, and workshop fabrication.

## Constraints

- Gear sizes needed to remain printable and large enough to house bearings.
- Shaft spacing needed to allow gear pairs to mesh without binding.
- The casing needed to support the shafts while staying understandable as a demonstration model.
- The synchroniser needed to slide across the axle and engage the selected gear pair.
- The project needed to fit school workshop timelines, material access, and OH&S rules.

## Final Outcome

The final model demonstrated many of the intended mechanical concepts, including gear rotation, gear-pair meshing, and synchroniser movement after modification. It did not fully meet the original output-speed target, because some planned ratios were removed after print-size and bearing-fit constraints were discovered.

## Recommended Improvements

- Rework the gear-ratio set with bearing fit and printable size checked before fabrication.
- Use straighter or stiffer shafts to reduce wobble and gear separation.
- Refine the synchroniser geometry so it can shift more smoothly while the axle is rotating.
- Add guards or clearer handling zones if the model is used regularly by younger students.
